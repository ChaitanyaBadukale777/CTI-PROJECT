Help Center

To protect your devices from cyber threats, ensure that your software is always up to date, use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and install trusted security software. Regularly back up your data and be cautious of phishing scams and suspicious links.

If you suspect a data breach, immediately change your passwords for affected accounts. Inform your IT team or security provider and monitor your accounts for any unusual activity. It’s important to run a full system scan with updated antivirus software and report the breach to relevant authorities.

Phishing attempts often come in the form of emails or messages that appear to be from trusted sources. Look out for suspicious URLs, unusual language, or unexpected requests for sensitive information. Always verify the sender's identity before clicking links or providing personal details.

Ransomware is a type of malware that locks your files or entire system until a ransom is paid. To prevent it, avoid clicking on unknown links, regularly back up your data, and use strong, unique passwords. Additionally, ensure your operating system and software have the latest security patches.

Signs of a compromised network include slow system performance, unexplained data usage, frequent crashes, and unfamiliar programs or files appearing. If you notice these signs, run security scans, review network activity logs, and consider consulting with a cybersecurity expert to assess the damage.

If you encounter a cyber attack or notice suspicious activity, it’s important to act quickly. First, disconnect affected devices from the internet to prevent further damage. Then, document all details of the incident, including timestamps, affected systems, and any potential signs of malicious behavior. Report the issue to your organization's IT or security team immediately, or contact your local law enforcement or cybersecurity agency. It’s essential to follow their instructions and cooperate with any investigations to help mitigate the impact.

Account Security

1. Account Security ensures that user accounts are protected from unauthorized access.
2. It involves implementing multi-factor authentication (MFA), strong password policies, and user monitoring.
3. Helps prevent identity theft, data breaches, and unauthorized access to sensitive information.
4. Critical for maintaining the integrity and confidentiality of systems.
5. Reduces the risk of insider threats and account compromises.

1. Used strong and unique passwords with a combination of letters, numbers and symbols.
2. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) using a second factor such as a security key or a mobile app.
3. Regularly update passwords and avoid using the same password across different platforms.
4. Avoid sharing login credentials with others.
5. Monitor account activity to detect suspicious behaviour.

1. Phishing Attacks: Hackers trick users into providing sensitive information.
2. Credential Stuffing: Using stolen usernames and passwords from data breaches.
3. Brute Force Attacks: Automated attempts to guess passwords.
4. Session Hijacking: Stealing a user’s active session to gain access.
5. Insider Threats: Malicious activity by users with legitimate access.

2. Implement role-based access control to limit user permissions.
3. Enable account lockouts after multiple failed login attempts.
